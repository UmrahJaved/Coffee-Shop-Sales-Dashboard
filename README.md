# Coffee-Shop-Sales-Dashboard
<h2>Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard</h2>

This project presents an interactive Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard designed to analyze sales performance across time, locations, and product categories.
The dashboard helps stakeholders understand customer behavior, revenue drivers, and operational patterns to support data-driven decision-making.

<img width="1895" height="762" alt="coffee_shop_sales_dashboard" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ab64fcd4-85ec-48b1-8705-7bee6bceff72" />

<h3>Objectives</h3>
1. Analyze overall sales and order trends
2. Identify peak hours and high-performing days
3. Compare store-level performance
4. Understand product category and beverage popularity
5. Provide actionable business insights

<h3>Key Insights</h3>
<h4>Overall Performance</h4>
<ul> 
<li>Generated $698.8K in total sales from 149,116 orders.</li>
<li> bill value is $4.69, indicating a high-volume, low-ticket sales model.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Time-Based Trends</h4>
<ul> 
<li>Peak sales occur between 7 AM – 10 AM, with highest demand around 9–10 AM.</li>
<li>Sales drop significantly after 11 AM and are lowest after 7 PM.</li>
<li>Business is heavily driven by morning commuter traffic.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Day-wise Analysis</h4>
<ul>
<li>Weekdays outperform weekends, with Monday, Thursday, and Friday leading in sales.</li>
<li>Saturday and Sunday show the lowest revenue contribution.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Monthly Sales Trend</h4>
<ul>
<li>Sales steadily increase from January to June.</li>
<li>February has the lowest sales, while June records the highest.</li>
<li>Indicates seasonality and growing customer engagement.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Store Performance</h4>
<ul>
<li>Hell’s Kitchen is the top-performing store (~$236.5K).</li>
<li>Astoria and Lower Manhattan follow closely with comparable performance.</li>
<li>Revenue differences suggest variation in average spend per transaction.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Product Category Distribution</h4>
<ul>
<li>Coffee (39%) is the primary revenue driver.</li>
<li>Tea (28%) and Bakery (12%) are strong secondary contributors.</li>
<li>Coffee and tea together account for 67% of total sales.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Top-Selling Beverages</h4>
<ul>
<li>Barista Espresso is the highest-selling product.</li>
<li>Brewed Chai Tea and Hot Chocolate perform strongly, highlighting demand beyond coffee.</li>
</ul>
  
<h3>Business Recommendations</h3>
<ul>
<li>Focus staffing and inventory planning around morning peak hours.</li>
<li>Introduce bundled offers (coffee + bakery) to increase average order value.</li>
<li>Launch weekend-specific promotions to improve underperforming days.</li>
<li>Replicate successful pricing or product strategies from Hell’s Kitchen across other stores.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Tools & Technologies</h3>

Data Visualization Tool: Excel (Pivot Tables, Measures)
Data Source: Coffee shop transactional sales data

<h3>Techniques Used:</h3>

1. Data aggregation
2. Time-series analysis
3. Category-wise distribution analysis
4. KPI tracking
